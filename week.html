WEEK 1

Marking CG on the chassis:

1.The material i'm using for chassis is acrylic having thickness of 4mm, wheel base is 15cm.

2.Locate the midpoint of wheelbase and the length of tha chassis.

3.Draw a line parallel to length passing through the midpoint and do the same for the wheelbase.

4.These lines are called the centre lines of the chassis.

5.The point of intersection of the centre lines is the CG of the chassis.

Fixing the motor and the rear axle on the chassis:

1.Fix the 15 teeth gear on motor by tightening the screw.

2.Now place the motor on the chassis such that the gear is aligned with the centre line.

3.We'll be fixing the motor with the help of U-clamp.For this firstly, put the clamp on the motor and mark the points for hole.

4.Using drillbit of 3mm, drill the holes and then tighten the screw properly(NOTE: Use washers also for proper fastening).

5.Cut a piece of chloroplast and place it under the motor to prevent the misalignment of motor on the chassis.

Now for the rear axle:

1.Measure the length of the shaft and mark its midpoint.

2.Measure the width of gear(say2.8cm).So its 1.4cm on either sides, mark these points.Place the gear properly and tigthen the screw.

3.Engage the gears properly and mark the points 1.5cm away from both the edges of the chassis as well as axle.

4.For fixing the bearings, put the sleeves on both the sides such that it sandwitches the bearing to prevent the lateral motion of the bearings.

5.While inserting the bearings their centre-line must align with the point marked on the chassis.

6.The sleeves were not fit enough to stop the lateral movement in my case.So, I used tape to increase the dia of the axle only upto the length required for the sleeve(not where bearings are placed).

7.Use a piece of chloroplast to prevent the sliding of bearings on the chassis.

8.Cut a 1cmx1cm of slot in the piece of chloroplast such that only top layer is removed.Use sharp tools carefully.I chopped my skin while making the slot.

9.Mesh the gears and place the rear axle such that gear is positioned left.

10.Place the sheet under the bearing and fix it on chassis using glue on all the four sides.

10.Mark the points on the chassis next to chloroplast benches in the line of bearings on both the sides.

11.Do make sure on the right side point is marked between the motor and bearings to make drilling process easy.

12.Drill 3mm hole at the points marked on the chassis.

13.Tighten the bearings a little using zip ties through the holes drilled.

Connecting Rod for suspension:

1.Take 15cm aluminium rod.Cut two 4cm pieces from it using jr. hacksaw.inish the edges using sandpaper.

2.Now on both the pieces mark a point(say A) at 1.1cm away from either side of the rod(say O).

3.Mark another point from A at 1cm(say B).

4.Now turn the rod at an angle of 90 and mark a point from O at 3.1cm(sayC).

5.Drill the holes at the marked points.Drilling will be easier if you flatten the rod a little.

6.At point A drill by using a drillbit of 2mm.

7.Drill other two holes by using a drillbit of 3mm.Remove the abrasives using sandpaper.

8.Take two 5cm copper connecting rod.Make it L-shaped at 1cm using nose-plier.

9.Insert this connecting rod in the 2mm hole drilled on the rod.

10.For fixing the connecting rod, mark two points at a distance of 5mm from the edges of the slot on both the sides of the chassis.

11.Drill the holes using 3mm drillbit.Fix the connecting rod using zip ties such that they are fixed with the chassis.

12.L-shaped bent must be towards the front end of the chassis.

13.The rod must be free to move after fixing of connecting rod.
